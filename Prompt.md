Build a web app called "Weimar Reimagined".

**Gallery.** Photos of real Weimar public spaces float and slowly drift across a dark screen, each slightly rotated. Click a photo to select it.

**Reimagine view.** Selected photo flies to fill the left half of the screen, fitting inside its container so the whole image is visible (letterboxed, not cropped). On the right: floating buttons to pick an architect (Lacaton & Vassal, Peter Zumthor, Álvaro Siza, Diébédo Francis Kéré, RCR Arquitectes, Grafton Architects, Louis Kahn, Carlo Scarpa) and style tags (more green, white render, raw concrete, all glass, rooftop terrace, car-free, night scene). Hit Generate to reimagine the space in that style using AI.

**Result.** Generated image crossfades in on the left. A toggle along the bottom of the image flips between original and generated. All results are saved and shown as a strip below the controls — a shared collection that grows with every generation.

**Info.** A small "i" button in the corner opens a short overlay explaining what the app is.
